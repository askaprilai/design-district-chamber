1 ACCEPT In the past month, when receiving concerning performance feedback about a team member, how often did you first seek to understand their perspective before forming conclusions?

2 ACCEPT Think of recent team interactions. How often do you catch yourself making assumptions about why someone acted a certain way, rather than asking clarifying questions?

Example: Someone misses a deadline, is late to meetings, seems disengaged, or makes mistakes.

3 ACCEPT When you hear statements like "Harleigh's office is not always neat and tidy, she's so not organized, I wonder what her house looks like" - how often do you recognize this as personal judgment rather than performance measurement?

4 ACCEPT During stressful periods or conflicts, how often do others comment that you remain calm and create a safe space for discussion?

5 ACCEPT How often do you find yourself being intentional about connecting with someone (understanding their feelings/perspective) rather than just communicating information?

Connection = seeking to understand their emotional state, concerns, perspectives first. Communication = sharing information, tasks, updates.

6 ACCEPT When someone doesn't follow through (like "When John doesn't call me back, it shows lack of care and follow up"), how often do you operate "above the line" by assuming responsibility rather than blaming?

Above the line = "What could I have done differently? How can I solve this?" Below the line = "They don't care, they're irresponsible, they always do this."

7 ACCEPT How often do you catch yourself using limiting self-talk ("I can't," "I don't know how," "I will never be able to") versus positive self-talk ("I can," "I will learn how," "I am able to")?

8 ACCEPT In difficult conversations, how often do you find yourself fully present (no multitasking, phone checking, or mental preparation of your response while they talk)?

9 CREATE Using the Eisenhower Matrix, how often do you find yourself working on "urgent but not important" tasks rather than "important but not urgent" strategic activities?

Urgent/Not Important = immediate demands, interruptions, some emails. Important/Not Urgent = strategic planning, relationship building, skill development, prevention.

10 CREATE How often do people comment that your energy and enthusiasm positively influences their motivation and engagement?

11 CREATE When delegating tasks, how often do you provide clear expectations, understand and validate their approach, and follow-up schedules without micromanaging the process?

12 CREATE How often do you actively challenge limiting beliefs in yourself and others (like "we've never done it that way" or "that won't work here")?

13 CREATE When things go wrong, how often do you immediately take ownership and focus on solutions rather than explaining external factors?

14 CREATE How often do you paint a compelling vision that connects individual work to broader organizational purpose and gets people excited about the future?

15 CREATE How often do you demonstrate emotional intelligence by reading situations and adapting your approach based on others' emotional states?

16 CREATE How often do you consider the broader impact of decisions on multiple stakeholders (not just your immediate team) before taking action?

17 TEACH When team members come to you with problems, how often do you use coaching questions to help them find solutions rather than immediately directing them what to do?

Coaching = "What do you think might work?" "What have you tried?" "What are your options?" Directing = "Here's what you need to do..." "The answer is..." "Just follow these steps..."

18 TEACH How often do you approach interactions with a coaching mindset (curious, positive, flexible) rather than just focusing on the technical skills of asking and listening?

Coaching Mindset = genuine curiosity about their growth, positive belief in their potential, flexibility to adapt approach. Technical Skills = just the mechanics of asking questions and listening.

19 TEACH When sharing knowledge or training others, how often do you focus on sharing wisdom and developing understanding rather than just "telling" information?

Training/Wisdom sharing = helping them understand the "why," asking questions, connecting to their experience. Telling = information dump, step-by-step instructions without context.

20 TEACH How often do you actively seek to learn from team members regardless of their position or experience level?

21 TEACH How often do you give specific, actionable feedback that includes both what they did well and how they can improve?

22 TEACH How often do you create stretch assignments or challenging opportunities that push team members beyond their current comfort zone?

23 TEACH How often do you model continuous learning by visibly pursuing your own development and sharing your learning journey with others?

24 SUPPORTIVE How often do you demonstrate genuine care and support for team members as whole people (not just their work performance)?

25 BE CORE When facing challenges, how often do you focus on solutions and what you can control rather than blaming external factors or circumstances?

26 BE CORE How often do you prioritize team and organizational success over your own personal recognition or advancement?

27 BE CORE How often do you invest significant time and energy in others' development even when it requires personal sacrifice or effort from you?

28 BE CORE How often do people tell you that you create a safe, supportive environment where they feel comfortable being vulnerable or admitting mistakes?

29 BE CORE When you make commitments or promises to team members, how often do you follow through completely and on time?

30 ACCOUNTABILITY How often do you have documented, specific, measurable expectations for each team member that you review regularly?

31 ACCOUNTABILITY How systematic and consistent are your follow-up processes for tracking commitments and progress?

32 ACCOUNTABILITY How consistently do you recognize and celebrate when team members meet or exceed their commitments?

33 ACCOUNTABILITY When someone fails to meet commitments, how quickly and effectively do you address the situation and reset expectations?

34 ACCOUNTABILITY How confident are you that each team member is in the right role that matches their strengths and abilities?

35 ACCOUNTABILITY How consistently available are you when team members need guidance, support, or have questions?
