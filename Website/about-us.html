About Us

Meet the visionary leaders driving innovation and excellence in Miami's Design District

April Sabral

Executive Director

April Sabral is a seasoned retail and hospitality leader with over three decades of global executive experience, having held leadership roles with Apple, Starbucks, Gap, and DAVIDsTEA. Starting as a part-time sales associate at 17, she rose to Senior Vice President overseeing 300 stores, building her career on developing teams, cultivating culture, and driving performance.

April lived in the Design District from 2003-2006 before leaving to follow her international retail career. She is so excited to be back and support the chamber as Executive Director. April's retail and hospitality background reflects the very spirit of the Design District—entrepreneurial, creative, and service-driven. Her mission is to champion local businesses, foster meaningful connections, and position the Design District as a thriving hub where commerce, culture, and innovation meet.

Board of Directors

Our distinguished board represents the diverse talents and expertise that make the Design District a world-class destination for creativity and commerce.

Lubna Nejjar

Chair

Award-winning CEO, Designer, Author & Retail Developer. Founder of IL Moda Brand with over a decade building 150+ fashion brands. Former Board President of Columbus Fashion Council.

Lubna Nejjar - Chair

Lubna Najjar is an accomplished entrepreneur, brand developer, and fashion designer, with over a decade of experience in the fashion and brand development industries. As the founder of IL Moda Brand, LLC (2012-present) and Her Denim, LLC, she has played a pivotal role in building and launching over 150 fashion brands, focusing on contemporary and influencer-led labels. Lubna was the curator and developer of the official Fashion District in downtown Columbus, Ohio, showcasing her ability to shape and drive industry initiatives on a large scale. Lubna now serves on the Board of Directors for the Design District Chamber of Commerce in Miami, FL, and BBB of South East Florida serving 240,000 businesses.

Beyond her work in brand development, Lubna has made significant contributions to fashion education as the co-author of the academic textbook *The Power of Branding in Fashion 2000 & Beyond*. Her leadership extends into industry governance, having served as the former Board President of the Columbus Fashion Council and as Chair of the Entrepreneurship Committee on the Better Business Bureau's Board of Directors. She is also a member of the Guild for the Council of Fashion Designers of America (CFDA).

Lubna's work has garnered widespread media attention, with features on *Good Morning America*, in *The Huffington Post*, *Forbes*, *USA Today*, and more, underscoring her influence and impact within the fashion industry.

Martu E. Freeman-Parker

Vice Chair

CEO/Founder of M.E.F. Productions, LLC. Fashion industry veteran with experience at Polo Ralph Lauren, J.Crew, and Calvin Klein. Currently Managing Director of Mana Fashion Services and board member of Miami Downtown Development Authority.

Martu E. Freeman-Parker - Vice Chair

Martu E. Freeman-Parker earned a BA in Fashion Merchandising/Consumer Services from Norfolk State University. She gained extensive experience in New York's apparel industry with nationally recognized companies including Polo Ralph Lauren, Calvin Klein Jeans, Gap Inc., Victoria Secret, and American Eagle Outfitters, holding positions from Assistant Production Manager to Design Coordinator.

Currently based in Miami, she operates M.E.F. Productions LLC, offering design coaching, line development, brand management, and fashion business workshops. Martu is the Founder & Host of Fashion TALKS Miami, a monthly networking event for Miami's local fashion community. In 2018, she published her first book "Get the Hell Out."

In October 2021, Martu became Managing Director of Mana Fashion Services, a subdivision of Mana Common in Miami. She serves on multiple boards including Miami Downtown Development Authority, Fashion Group International, and St Thomas University Fashion Merchandising Program.

Atiba Madyun

Treasurer

Over 25 years building coalitions between corporations, nonprofits, and government bodies. Former Deputy Executive Director of NBCSL and policy consultant.

Atiba Madyun - Treasurer

Atiba has over 25 years of experience building coalitions between corporations, nonprofits, government bodies, labor unions, advocacy groups, and the private sector, with a specialty in legislative strategy, communication, community organizing, outreach, and education. He has been on numerous boards including CommonHealth Action and served as treasurer, then chaired the Office of the District of Columbia's Mayor's Commission on National and Community Service, now ServeDC.

From 2002-2009, Atiba was the Deputy Executive Director overseeing the policy staff for the National Black Caucus of State Legislators (NBCSL). In this role, he opened relations with the Bush White House, created an annual meeting between NBCSL and the Congressional Black Caucus (CBC), wrote two award-winning grant proposals and worked on what is now an ongoing partnership with the National Hispanic Caucus of State Legislators (NHCSL). This partnership grew to include the Native American Caucus of State Legislators and the National Caucus of Asian Pacific Islanders in what is now referred to as the Quad Caucus.

His work as a consultant and an intermediary with numerous policymaking organizations includes the National Conference of State Legislators, Council of State Governments, National Governors Association, Congressional Black Caucus among others. He worked more recently with Concepts Communications on the State Exchange on Employment and Disability (S.E.E.D.) through the Department of Labor's Office of Disability Employment Policy on strategic outreach to policymakers and policy organizations.

Olga Largen

Board Member

Nonprofit sector leader with a decade of experience in fundraising, donor relations, and social impact. Passionate advocate for diversity, inclusion, and social justice with certifications in child advocacy and housing equity.

Olga Largen - Board Member

With a decade of work experience in the nonprofit sector, Olga is passionate about creating positive social change and empowering vulnerable populations. She manages and cultivates relationships with individual and corporate donors, coordinates fundraising campaigns and events, and communicates the impact of programs and services.

Olga serves as a member of the board of advisors for the Interfaith Advisory Board, where she collaborates with leaders from different faith communities to foster dialogue, understanding, and cooperation. She holds multiple certifications, including child advocate, notary public, and housing equity, advocacy, and leadership.

She has strong skills in project management, communication, and training, which she uses to support the mission and vision of the organizations she works with. Olga is fluent in English, Spanish, and Italian, and enjoys learning about different cultures and perspectives. Her areas of focus include diversity, inclusion, and social justice.

Thomas McClure

Secretary

Founder of Columbus Fashion Council and Fashion Week Columbus. Cultural strategist with 14+ years building platforms for creative economies and supporting emerging designers in fashion industry development.

Thomas McClure - Secretary

Thomas McClure is the founder of the Columbus Fashion Council, a non-profit organization dedicated to supporting and developing the fashion industry and community in Columbus, Ohio. The organization also oversees Fashion Week Columbus, which McClure founded in 2010.

As a founder and cultural strategist, McClure has built platforms to foster creative economies. He spearheaded the development of nationally recognized fashion programming and nonprofit infrastructure, supporting emerging designers and contributing to economic growth and community value in the Columbus fashion industry.

He also served as the Executive Director of the Greater Columbus Film Commission for a period. In May 2023, after 14 years, he stepped down as Executive Director of the Columbus Fashion Council. As of January 2025, he has taken on the role of Interim Executive Director for the Columbus Fashion Council.

Yolanda Vega

Board Member

Director of Stores at Zadig & Voltaire with 20+ years in luxury retail. Strategic leader specializing in operational excellence, talent development, and customer experience innovation.

Yolanda Vega - Board Member

Yolanda Vega is a dynamic retail executive and strategic leader with over 20 years of experience driving business growth in the luxury and contemporary fashion sectors. As Director of Stores at Zadig & Voltaire, she oversees high-volume, high-visibility markets, developing top-performing teams that deliver exceptional results.

Committed to building strong leadership pipelines and fostering diverse, customer-centric cultures, Yolanda is dedicated to influencing the industry and her community at a broader level.
