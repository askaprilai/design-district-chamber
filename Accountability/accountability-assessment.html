Step 1: Right Person, Right Role

Ensuring you have the right people in positions that match their strengths and capabilities

1Honestly: When you need something done, how often do you actually match the person to the role vs. just assigning it to whoever's available?

Step 2: Expectations Are Clear & Confirmed

Ensuring all expectations are crystal clear, documented, and mutually understood

2Be honest: How often do you actually have your team validate understanding by showing you what they heard (even when you're rushed or they're experienced)?

Step 3: Agreed Consequences for Missed Expectations

Establishing mutual agreement on what happens when expectations aren't met

3How well do you establish agreed-upon consequences for missed expectations?

Step 4: Follow-Up Plan Locked In

Creating detailed plans for monitoring progress and checking in on commitments

4How detailed and consistent are your follow-up plans for tracking progress?

Step 5: Course-Correct Quickly

Making rapid adjustments when things aren't going according to plan

5Reality check: When you see someone missing expectations, how quickly do you actually address it (not just think about addressing it)?

Step 6: Show Up Consistently

Being present, engaged, and reliable in your leadership role

6How consistently do you show up and stay involved with your team?

Step 7: Clarify Before You Assume

Seeking understanding and confirmation rather than making assumptions

7How often do you clarify understanding rather than make assumptions?

Step 8: Celebrate What's Working

Recognizing and acknowledging good performance and positive outcomes

8How well do you recognize and celebrate good performance?
